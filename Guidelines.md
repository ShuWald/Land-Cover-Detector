GDG Challenge Guidelines, for reference

## Project Setup
● Problem Discovery: Did the team demonstrate a structured approach to problem-solving, including thorough research and
analysis of the business challenge?
● Unique Perspective: Does the solution offer a new approach or perspective to the problem? Is it unique compared to
existing solutions?
## Implementation
● Technical Execution: Is the technical implementation well-executed with proper coding, design, and architecture?
● Google Technology: Does the solution effectively leverage Google technology in a creative and meaningful way to address
the core problem?
● Visual Communication: Are diagrams, mockups, or prototypes used effectively to communicate the architecture and user
flow of the solution?
## Feedback, Testing & Iteration
● Validation: Did the team demonstrate a structured approach to testing their solution against the intended use case?
● Problem Alignment: How well does the solution actually solve the stated business problem in a functional, real-world
scenario?
## Success and Completion of Solution
● Business Value: Does the solution offer tangible benefits to the business (e.g., increased efficiency, cost savings, or
improved customer experience)?
● Quantifiable Impact: Does the team describe the success of their solution using clear metrics, goals, outcomes, or
cause-and-effect relationships?
● Resource Optimization: Does the solution optimize resources such as time, cost, and labor?
● Presentation Quality: Is the solution explained in a clear, concise manner? Is the final presentation persuasive, engaging,
and easy to understand?
